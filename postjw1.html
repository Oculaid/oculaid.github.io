Initial Mechanical Renderings

Take a look at the initial OculAid renderings, pre-fabrication!

Posted by Jacob Wehrle on January 21, 2021

OculAid Mechanical Vision

The mechanical assembly consists of 3 main sub-assemblies which are the front frame, and the two arms. For mechanical and electrical robustness, the arms of the OculAid will not fold in like typical glasses. The circuit board has been placed on the front frame in order to give the sensors an optimal view of the environment in front of the user.

As a result of this, shown below, the batteries have been relocated onto the side arms to help distribute the weight away from the nose, as well as for aesthetics. For feedback, there is a vibration motor at the back of each arm, which vibrates based on the proximity and location of detected objects. The arms were designed to fit tightly to the head behind the ears to provide a consistant vibratory feedback.

There are three cutouts on the top of the glasses frame: The two outer cutouts are for the 3 pole switches, which are in place for sensor adjustability And the center hole is used for accessing the MicroUSB charging port

Below, you can see the general assembly of the Oculaid In the final prototype of the OculAid, each of the exposed faces will be covered for aesthetic and water-proofing reasons. The overall mechanical design was created to adhere to the ISO 12312-1 Standard for sunglasses and protective eyewear. This mainly pertains to the overall comfortability of the glasses rather than the UV protection which is also detailed in those standards.
