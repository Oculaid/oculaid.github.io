About OculAid

Take Back Your Independence

Visually impaired individuals around the world use the white cane or guide dogs to be able to scan their surrounding area to detect any obstacles or orientation markers on the ground. However, these solutions are primarily used to detect objects below the waist level in the userâ€™s immediate path. As a result, 88% of surveyed legally blind individuals and 98% of blind individuals have experienced head-level accidents, caused by bumping into things at chest height or higher. 86% of these head-level injuries occurred while outdoors. Further, 23% of these head-level injuries required medical attention, with 60% requiring a medical professional to assist. 43% of individuals that experienced these accidents changed their walking habits as a way to avoid these accidents, and 26% of these individuals reported that after these accidents they lost confidence in their ability to travel independently.

A need exists for a device to aid visually impaired individuals, alongside their existing mobility tools, that provides them with the ability to detect objects from waist to above head-level. This would allow them to avoid these head-level accidents and regain their confidence to travel independently without a sighted companion. Further, this device should be low-cost so that it can be affordable and available to working class individuals.

The team at OculAid is working to create a solution to allow blind individuals to detect obstacles above waist level. The solution uses SensorFusion to merge measurements from a multitude of sensors including ultrasonic and infrared sensors to detect these objects. Easy and intuitive adjustability will allow users to adapt on the fly and regain their independence.
