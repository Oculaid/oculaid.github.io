Electronics Finalization and PCB Order

Finalization of the electronic system schematics, board layout and component placement and ordering of the PCBs.

Posted by David Beckett on February 16, 2021

Schematics

The below schematic shows the power control circuitry. It consists of the 2 pairs of lithium ion batteries, the lithium ion charger, the 5V boost converter and the 3.3V LDO voltage regulator. A power switch was added to the enable pin of the boost convertor, this will shut off the 5V output to power off the circuitry.

The below schematic shows the different sensors and actuators present in the system. It shows the three HCSR04 ultrasonic sensors, the two Sharp IR sensors, the two vibration modules and finally the two control switches. The two SP3T switches have the three output terminals connected to the three nodes of a voltage divider to provide three distinct readings to the analog input.

The below schematic shows the IMU, a seperate 3.3V voltage regulator is used to isolate the control voltage for the IMU which helps isolate the the power for this circuitry.

The final schematic below shwos the MCU, this schematic was created referencing the schematic for the STM Nucleo dev boards. The header P5 is used for connecting the programmer/debugger to the board.

PCB Layout

The below two images show the 2D and 3D layout of thr boards. This shows with all of the components and all of the traces routed on the board. the ultrasonic sensor sit slightly off the board so that other surface mount components are placed underneath.
